---
title: "The Uneconomical State of TWS"
date: 2023-02-22T01:00:39+01:00
tags: ["Audio"]
---
So called TWS (True Wireless Stereo) earbuds
are powered by lithium-ion batteries and Bluetooth.
What makes them highly impractical for me is that,
as with any other product with a lithium-ion battery,
they become useless after some years, as the aforementioned 
battery degrades over time. So lets say you spend $150
on some wireless earbuds with Active Noise Cancelling.
Now after lets say three years the battery has degraded at
least so much that you consider getting new ones, so you're
essentially spending $50 per year. Some wired earbuds with
an acceptable sound quality can be bought for as little as
$20. And those may last longer than a year. So the cost per
year is in any case going to be substantially lower. The
most convincing advantage is the Active Noise Cancelling,
but at that price difference I am not sure if they are worth 
the extra cost. One has to recall that we don't spend most
of our times in airplanes, because that's where those
ANC earbuds do really shine. Where wired earbuds really
shine is the fact that they always work (except when they
are broken). They never need a charge. They are small and
portable and never need to be charged, that's what makes
them so great. Wireless earbuds aren't bad, they are just
not very economical.
